How I Work:
Four Moves.

01 Map the Landscape
Read more arrow_forward
keyboard_arrow_up
People don't do what they say they do.
People don't do what they think they do.
People don't do what we think they do.
— IDEO
Why
In enterprise, the scope is vast. What any individual sees is usually the tip of the iceberg. Map the full picture before we make any major decision.
What
Show the totality of an ecosystem — team structures, core entities, main workflows, existing pain points. The goal is a shared artifact that lets everyone see the same big picture.
How
Combine quantitative data (usage logs, volume) with qualitative research (shadowing, interviews). Big data tells us where to look, observations tells us what's actually happening.
Case Study
Asurion has nearly 10,000 support agents worldwide. Each meets with a team coach weekly to improve performance — but coaches consistently reported that their tools gave them too much data and not enough insight. No one had ever mapped what their actual workflow and their decision-making process looked like. That's where I came in.
Case Study Cover
Case Study
800+ rules govern every AI-assisted review in the home appraisal pipeline — but their content, logic, and relationships were scattered across spreadsheets, dashboards, and hard code. No single source of truth. Every new client onboarding, every rule change, every quality check required someone to manually trace dependencies across systems. We reached a tipping point and had to redesign a holistic rule management system.

02 Build Shared Understanding
Read more arrow_forward
keyboard_arrow_up
Why
Get reactions early. Every stakeholder brings a different slice of the picture — surfacing those perspectives early prevents costly misalignment down the road.
What
Frequent, bite-size sharing — not big reveals. The goal is to build a shared understanding incrementally, so there are no surprises when it's time to make decisions.
How
Debriefs during standups, pop-up shares in Slack, and formal readouts with key stakeholders. Match the format to the audience and the stakes.

Why

04 Validate Early
Read more arrow_forward
keyboard_arrow_up
Fail early. Fail often. Fail forward.
Why
No matter how much we pressure-test internally, things never work out 100% as planned. Finding out in production is costly. Validate before it becomes a commitment.
What
Rapid, real-world feedback from the people who will actually use the product — not stakeholders reviewing mockups, but end users reacting to concepts.
How
Build lasting relationships with end users. Assemble diverse panels to give rapid feedback on concepts and ideas before they become commitments.
Examples
